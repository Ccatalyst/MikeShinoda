I love Mike Shinoda but hate NFTs


Mike Shinoda has put out a variety of great music over the years, from his time in Linkin Park and heading Fort Minor to his independant albums and lofi instrumental tracks. Mike Shinoda is also a big fan of video games, and shares his video game love on his Twitter. Recently his love of video games has been tainted by the foul stench of Non-fungible Tokens, or NFTs.

Biography

Mike Shinoda is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, multi-instrumentalist, record producer, and graphic designer. He co-founded Linkin Park in 1996 and is the band's lead vocalist, as well as rhythm guitarist, keyboardist, primary songwriter and producer. Shinoda later created a hip-hop-driven side project, Fort Minor, in 2004. He has also served as a producer for tracks and albums by Lupe Fiasco, Styles of Beyond and the X-Ecutioners.

Why are NFTs a problem?

NFTs have a plethora of problems:

  • The energy used to "mint" an NFT is high, causing huge draws on power grids in various countries, causing shortages and blackouts.
  • The desire for hardware used to creat the blockchain for NFTs has created shortages in the market
  • Because it is purchased using anonymous currency, scams and thefts are prevalent
